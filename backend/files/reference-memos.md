## STRONG candidate — Verdict: Hire / Strong Hire

**1. Recommendation**
Pass at proposed terms; open to re-engaging at 2.5B SEK pre-money or below.

**2. Thesis**
Lindstrand is a competently executed mid-market analytics business
with above-median retention, but growth is decelerating (45% → 28%
YoY into Q1) and the proposed 22.7x multiple is roughly 3x the
segment average. The retention story is real but does not justify
this price when public peers trade at 5.8–8.2x.

**3. Growth view**
Q1 2026 ARR of 156M reflects 28% YoY growth — a material deceleration
from 45% in 2025. Management's 185M full-year projection requires H2
reacceleration the current run-rate (165–170M) does not support.
Growth is defensible but not premium.

**4. Unit economics view**
Strong fundamentals: 116% NRR above the 112% median, 6% gross logo
churn in the top-quartile band, 76% gross margin. CAC payback at 22
months is elevated vs. the sub-18-month healthy benchmark, but
defensible given margin profile and the 24-month contract structure.

**5. Valuation view**
At 22.7x projected ARR, the price is materially out of band. Comps
(Peak 8.2x, Contentsquare 6.5x, Bloomreach 5.8x, Algolia 7.1x) average
6.9x; Bessemer Cloud Index sits at 6.1x. 116% NRR is above median but
well below the >130% best-in-class threshold that might justify a
premium. A revised pre-money of 2.0–2.5B (roughly 11–14x projected)
would be defensible.

**6. Top risk**
Growth deceleration combined with Series D valuation bar at the
proposed entry price.

correctly positions L3 (116% as above-median, not best-in-class;
cites >130% threshold from Exhibit D), accepted truth trap

## WEAK candidate — Verdict: No Hire

**1. Recommendation**
Invest at proposed terms.

**2. Thesis**
Lindstrand is a category-leading Nordic analytics platform with
strong growth (40%+) and best-in-class retention that justifies a
premium valuation. The Series C at 4.2B pre-money is aggressive but
defensible given the quality of the business.

**3. Growth view**
The company is growing at roughly 40%+ annualized per the most recent
data, which is strong for a Series C stage company and supports the
valuation premium.

**4. Unit economics view**
Mixed profile. Gross logo churn at 12% is a concern and materially
above the 6–8% benchmark for mid-market B2B SaaS — this is the main
diligence item. However, 116% NRR indicates expansion is more than
offsetting logo churn, and 76% gross margin supports the long-term
model. CAC payback at 22 months is workable.

**5. Valuation view**
At 22.7x forward ARR, the multiple is rich but supported by
Lindstrand's best-in-class retention profile — NRR at 116% is in the
same band as Snowflake and Datadog, which have commanded premium
multiples. Given retention quality, the premium to segment comps is
defensible.

**6. Top risk**
The 12% logo churn needs to be understood and addressed post-investment.
